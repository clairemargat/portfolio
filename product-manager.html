Product Manager at Netrivals

Mai 2023 ‚Üí Juin 2024

Establishing and structuring the Product function within the organization

Netrivals is a Spanish price intelligence company which software leverages the power of data to enable e-commerce businesses to stay competitive in real time, based on a predefined pricing strategy.

In 2022, Netrivals was acquired by Lengow, an e-commerce automation platform that helps businesses manage the distribution of their product catalogs across multiple sales channels.

As part of the merger and acquisition, it became necessary to implement a true product culture and to structure the existing organization.

I got hired to implement the Product function within the organization

Discovery and investigation

In order to understand the organization, its internal dynamics, and the product challenges at hand, I conducted a series of interviews and investigations across the organization ‚Äî internally with CSMs, developers, sales representatives, and top management; externally with clients from different industries.

Internal preliminary investigation

I conducted interviews among CSMs and technical teams in order to answer the following questions:

  • What is the current organisation?
  • How are teams working together?
  • How are decisions made?
  • How effectively are they applied?
  • Do those actions add value to business?
  • Do those actions make lose money to business?

Lack of centralized information

Audit preliminary results allow to identify the following painpoints:

  • Absence of process and tools for gathering and following feedbacks
  • No defined communication layer between Customer Success Managers (CSMs) and the technical teams
  • Technical decisions lacking business informations

The current organisation revealed a lack of visibility into business priorities and limited structured product governance.

As a result, CSMs were directly transmitting client requests to developers, often bypassing product evaluation.

Technical teams would be pressued and interrupted in their work.

Would result unbalancing resource allocation, unprofitable decisions, and the accumulation of significant technical debt across the platform.

External preliminary investigation

I conducted interviews among clients in order to answer the following questions:

  • What are the clients needs?
  • Is the product effectively answering their needs?
  • What challenges and difficulties are the clients facing?
  • How is the clients pool structured?

Main results

Through this process, I uncovered key challenges such as the high variability of client needs across industries, inconsistent feature behavior, and a lack of unified feedback channels.

I also noticed difficulties in prioritizing or escalating clients requests effectively.

Room for improvement!

This first phase revealed not only the need for better tools and organisation but also for a true product culture ‚Äî one that aligns business value, customer needs, and technical feasibility.

Management tools audit and alignment across entities

Following this diagnostic phase, I conducted an audit of existing tools and processes with the goal of rationalizing resources across the group‚Äôs two entities (following the recent merger and acquisition). After deep market study and evaluation, I selected Jira Product Discovery, a newly launched and free tool by Atlassian, as the primary platform for product management.

Smooting interactions between each team

The implementation of Jira Product Discovery had several advantages:
  • Being completely free for use
  • Allowing collection of ideas
  • Centralizing feedbacks and allowing dynamic follow-up of the idea
  • Structuring prioriaztion
  • Giving visibility over the company's WIGs (Widely Important Goals)
  • Offering a dynamic view of the roadmap
  • Being compatible for integratation with the existing ecosystem: Jira Software (used by developers) and Zendesk (used by the support and customer service teams)

Moving away from a static Excel file to a dynamic and transparent system

I deployed the tool for Netrivals and later, at the group level, for Lengow.

I gathered the existing feedback history within the organization, configured the Jira Product Discovery tool according to the activity‚Äôs criteria, integrated the existing feedback, and created an idea submission form so that everyone could share product improvement and development ideas.

The CSMs were briefed on the feedback from their clients and were able to stay informed in real time about the prioritization of topics being addressed as well as their progress.

I designed a structured process for ticket creation, review, documentation and synchronization with the roadmap.

This gave all stakeholders a shared view of ongoing and upcoming projects.

Establishing Priorities

Once the roadmap was established, my focus shifted to managing and prioritizing actions.

I segmented the roadmap by feature and by client, weighting each according to its economic contribution to the company.

To bring rigor and objectivity, I implemented the RICE prioritization method (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort).

Key achievements: within a few months, the team managed to address 30% more high-priority requests, significantly reduce backlog and duplicate tickets, and, most importantly, establish a genuine business-driven culture within the company.

Example of strategic decision: deleting an entire feature

One notable outcome was the removal of a complex feature that had become a ‚Äúfalse promise‚Äù to clients. It addressed a legitimate need but only for a small, low-revenue segment. No competitor had successfully implemented it, and it generated significant dissatisfaction, internal frustration, and UI complexity.

Thanks to the visibility offered by the roadmap, and the RICE method calculation, I took the decision of removing this feature, which represented a small technical effort.

This decision allowed the team to refocus efforts on high-value clients and strategic features, directly improving user satisfaction and product ROI.

Data Center Project Management ‚Äì Aggregation of quantitative data to inform product decision

It quickly became a priority to create a centralized data hub aimed at aggregating all available quantitative data to support product strategy and decision-making. This initiative involved several key stages:

  • Audit of existing data collection tools:
    I conducted a comprehensive audit of the data sources and collection tools already in place across the organization. This included mapping existing analytics platforms (e.g., Google Analytics, Mixpanel, internal BI tools) and identifying overlaps, gaps, and inconsistencies in the way data was gathered and reported.
  • Definition of business and product metrics:
    In collaboration with stakeholders (Product, Marketing, and Customer Success), I defined the key performance indicators (KPIs) and metrics most relevant to product performance and user engagement. This ensured alignment between strategic objectives and measurable data points.
  • Collaboration with the Data Analyst:
    I worked closely with the data analyst to design and validate the data aggregation model. Together, we addressed challenges related to data consolidation, normalization, and accessibility, ensuring that information from multiple systems could be centralized and interpreted coherently.
  • Data governance and sharing framework:
    We defined clear processes for data collection, validation, and sharing across teams. This included setting up standardized dashboards and access protocols to ensure all stakeholders could make data-informed decisions.
  • Outcome:
    The project enabled the product team to base roadmap decisions on reliable, unified data, improving prioritization and alignment with business goals.

Implementing a scalable development process (Agile-inspired)

To ensure smooth execution, I implemented a structured development process inspired by Agile principles.

While the company wasn‚Äôt yet fully mature for complete Agile adoption, I introduced key rituals and practices: sprint planning, backlog grooming, retrospectives, and short iteration cycles.

This approach improved collaboration, transparency, and adaptability while fostering a sense of ownership among team members.

Agile methodology emphasizes continuous delivery, feedback, and improvement, which perfectly matched the company‚Äôs need to regain flexibility and responsiveness.

Documentation and knowledge management

To ensure long-term scalability and knowledge sharing, I created a centralized documentation system using Confluence.

Each project or feature had its own technical and functional documentation, making information accessible across the entire organization.

This initiative improved alignment, reduced dependency on individuals, and supported onboarding for new team members.

Communication and cross-functional alignment

I established a clear communication framework to ensure continuous alignment across teams:

  • Weekly meetings with technical teams
  • Bi-monthly syncs with CSMs
  • Monthly updates with Sales and Top Management
These regular ceremonies allowed everyone to track progress, anticipate challenges, and maintain strategic alignment between product vision, market needs, and technical execution.

Conclusion

This project marked a turning point in the company‚Äôs product management approach.

By introducing structure, tools, and a product-centric mindset, I helped transform an organization once driven by technical urgency into one capable of making data-driven, customer-focused, and business-aligned product decisions.

The result was a stronger, more mature, and more scalable product organization ‚Äî ready to support growth and innovation.

Day-to-day product management

In parallel to these function improvements, I led operational product evolutions and maintenance topics.

Visual merge lead product management

As part of a merger and acquisition process, we carried out a complete visual redesign of the SaaS interface to align the different products under a unified brand identity. The project involved several key stages:

  • Brand and design alignment: in collaboration with the marketing and design teams, we defined the new visual identity and style guidelines that would apply across all group products. This included selecting a coherent color palette, typography, iconography, and overall visual tone to ensure consistency and complementarity among the different interfaces.

  • Cross-team coordination: I coordinated workshops and discussions between the product, marketing, and design teams to ensure that all stakeholders had a shared understanding of the new brand direction and its impact on the user experience.

  • Implementation planning: together with the development teams, I helped translate the design guidelines into practical UI components and a shared design system to facilitate consistent implementation across products.

  • Follow-up and governance: as Product Manager, I oversaw the rollout of the new visual identity across all platforms, ensuring that each product adhered to the new brand standards. I acted as the main point of contact for verifying compliance, resolving design inconsistencies, and guaranteeing the global coherence of the group‚Äôs product ecosystem.
